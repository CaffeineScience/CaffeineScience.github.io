Caffeine

C8H10N4O2

1,3,7-trimethylpurine-2,6-dione

Caffeine is a commonly used stimulant in coffee and tea products. This is due to its stimulating effect on the nervous system, heart, and kidneys. Caffeine causes increase in motor performance, increase in alertness, and decreased fatigue. Caffeine plants are indigenous to the Ethiopian region and have spread throughout the world since the 4th century. Tea has been used in China for thousands of years and caffeine was often used to stay awake during prayers and other religious events. Today caffeine is very important in many Americans' daily lives to keep them awake during the day through coffee and caffeine is commonly used in sodas as well.
